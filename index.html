ANEMIA IN CHILDREN

This disease is related to poor brain development that will result in poor performance in school learning. Anemia, not only limits the physical, social and emotional activity of the child, but also affects their ability to learn and retain memory. 

IDENTIFICATION OF EFFECTS

SHORT TERM

  •  Child development is a process of continuous changes in the child. In this analysis, attention is focused on only three of the areas of child development, in which the association that exists with anemia in the first stages of life has been widely documented; These areas are the mental, motor and behavioral development of the individual (Grantham-McGregor and Ani, 2001)
  • Iron plays a fundamental role in the synthesis process of neurotransmitters, so that the low reserve of this in the first years of life, is related to neuropsychological alterations, such as the delay in psychomotor developments and alterations in their psychological stability and in the low intellectual performance. (Murray, 2013)
  • Muñoz (2012) mentions that "among other immediate consequences of anemia are growth retardation, decreased immune response, regulation of altered temperature, signs of fatigue, weakness and pallor, as well as irritability and attention deficit" (pp. 825-835).

IN BRAIN DEVELOPMENT

  • Many of the studies on the association of iron status in early childhood and psychomotor and behavioral development do not differentiate between iron deficiency anemia and iron deficiency (Murray-Kolb L, 2013).
  • Although we know that iron is a good part of the proteins that are responsible for the transport of oxygen, hemoglobin and myoglobin, a small but important percentage of iron is part of other enzymes and key compounds. This is a mineral content in enzymes that participate in the synthesis of lipids that, in turn, are for cell membranes and for the synthesis of honey in the brain and therefore, cognitive and motor functions become negatively before a problem myelination (Murray-Kolb LE, 2013).
  • Iron is also part of the metabolic processes of neurotransmitters mainly dopaminergic and serotoninergic that would have an impact on behavior. All these processes are related to the hippocampus, the brain region and learning and memory, as well as affective behaviors such as depression and anxiety.

IN MOTOR DEVELOPMENT

Frongillo, Tofail, Hamadani, Warren and Mehrin mention that "the motor area is related to the ability of children to control the movement of their muscles, classifying them into fine and gross motor skills" (2014, pp. 68-88).

In 2001 a systematic review was carried out and it was found that infants who suffered from iron deficiency anemia in their first years of life developed a low motor system (Szajewska, Ruszcynski and Chmielewska, 2010).

Subsequently, in 2010, a review was conducted in which it was found that iron supplementation in children has a positive influence on their psychomotor development, but no influence was found on their mental development and behavior, as shown in the following table. It should be noted that motor development is important to subsequently execute reading and writing functions, which involve both cognitive and motor areas (Szajewska, Ruszczynski and Chmielewska, 2010).

IN BEHAVIOR

  • In children who suffered from anemia due to iron deficiency in their first year of life and that was later corrected by iron supplementation, it was found that, after 10 years of follow-up, they had longer reaction times and a lower ability to control impulsive responses. , what is called inhibitory control (Algarín, Nelson, Peirano, Westerlund, Reyes and Lozoff, 2013). This difficulty is one of the behavioral deficits that is related to disorders such as attention deficit and hyperactivity, among others.
  • Frongillo et al. (2014) mention that: "socio-emotional behavior is another area of ​​child development, and includes the establishment of relationships with other people using their emotions, as well as the regulation of himself and his interest to the outside" (page 68- 88).
  • These inappropriate behaviors and behavioral problems have an impact on home, school and work.
  • On the other hand, a study in China found that low levels of hemoglobin in four-year-old children were associated with problems of externalized behavior at age 6, an association that was not affected by problems of social adversity. There was also a difference according to sex in the behavioral manifestation at six years, children had more attention problems, while girls had greater aggression. This study has some limitations such as the small sample size, reports only low levels of hemoglobin and not anemia, and does not mention other measures that assess malnutrition such as weight and height. Among the limitations of the systematic reviews we can mention that they combined many types of evaluations (mental and motor), others did not consider the age difference, nor the instruments with which the mental or motor development was evaluated.
  • For several decades it has been known that iron deficiency negatively impacts psychomotor and behavioral performance. Some studies suggest that these effects could have a long-term impact, that it is not possible to reverse despite having overcome anemia, however, there is no consensus on this and more research is required (Su, Cui, Zhou, Ai, Sun and Zhao, 2016).

LONG TERM

DEPRESSION
In studies performed in patients on hemodialysis, it has been shown that anemia directly affects brain function and is responsible for cognitive impairments. The reduction of muscle strength and fatigue are commonly associated with anemia and can have detrimental effects on the quality of life of the participants, which facilitates the development of depressive symptoms. Thus, anemia influences emotional state of the person. (Onder, 2005)

LABOR PRODUCTIVITY
People with iron deficiency anemia are characterized by fatigue and weakness. There has been a constant accumulation of evidence that iron deficiency anemia limits maximum physical performance, submaximal resistance and spontaneous activity in the adult, resulting in a decrease in labor productivity with consequent economic losses. (Lozoff, 1988)

SALARY & POVERTY
Iron-deficiency anemia could perpetuate a cycle of poor production and labor productivity, difficulty maintaining a job, difficulties in continuing training and education, and this income would make it difficult for women to obtain the types of foods that are rich in iron: meat, birds and fish. Insufficient intake of iron-rich foods may predispose to the development or exacerbation of iron deficiency and iron-deficiency anemia. (Semba, 2003)
