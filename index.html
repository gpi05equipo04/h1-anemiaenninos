ANEMIA IN CHILDREN

This disease is related to poor brain development that will result in poor performance in school learning. Anemia, not only limits the physical, social and emotional activity of the child, but also affects their ability to learn and retain memory.

IDENTIFICATION OF EFFECTS

  • PHYCHOMOTOR DEVELOPMENT: Child development is a process of continuous changes in the child. In this analysis, attention is focused on only three of the areas of child development, in which the association that exists with anemia in the first stages of life has been widely documented; These areas are the mental, motor and behavioral development of the individual. 

In 2010, a review was conducted in which it was found that iron supplementation in children has a positive influence on their psychomotor development, but no influence was found on their mental development and behavior. It should be noted that motor development is important to subsequently execute reading and writing functions, which involve both cognitive and motor areas.
